# Empirical Sources for Social Epidemiology Article: Polish Caregiving Asymmetry

## Executive Summary

This research identified authoritative sources for 6 of 10 requested empirical claims. **Critical gaps exist** for three high-priority items: age-disaggregated Polish time-use data, the Suwada 88.7% gatekeeping statistic, and joint physical custody rates. Additionally, the reported 19% gender wage gap is **incorrect**—Poland's actual gap is 4.5%, among the EU's lowest.

## 3. POLISH PARENTAL LEAVE POLICY SPECIFICS

### STATUS: COMPLETE - Exact specifications confirmed

### Maternity Leave (urlop macierzyński)

**Duration:** 20 weeks for single birth (100% earnings)
- Mandatory period: 14 weeks post-birth (mother only)
- Transferable: 6 weeks can be transferred to father after mandatory period
- Multiple births: 31-37 weeks depending on number

### Paternity Leave (urlop ojcowski)

**Duration:** 2 weeks (14 days)
- Payment: 100% of average earnings
- **Non-transferable:** Individual right of father
- Time limit: Must be taken within 24 months after birth

### Parental Leave (urlop rodzicielski) - Current System

**Pre-April 2023:**
- Duration: 32 weeks for single child
- Payment: 60% earnings (or 80% if chosen at maternity leave start)
- **Total combined leave: 52 weeks** (20 maternity + 32 parental)
- Non-transferable portion: None (fully shareable)

**Post-April 2023 (EU Directive Implementation):**
- Duration: 41 weeks for single child  
- Payment: 70% earnings
- **Total combined leave: 61 weeks** (20 maternity + 41 parental)
- **Non-transferable: 9 weeks reserved for each parent**

### 2016 Reform Details

The 2016 reform (effective January 2, 2016) **reclassified** rather than expanded leave:
- Maternity leave: Reduced from 26 to 20 weeks
- Parental leave: Increased from 26 to 32 weeks
- Total: Remained 52 weeks
- Rationale: Make leave more shareable by shifting 6 weeks from maternity (mother-only) to parental (shareable) category

### Father Uptake Rates

**Paternity Leave (2-week non-transferable):**
- **2016:** 146,400 fathers; average 13.2 days used (94% of entitlement)
- **2023:** 204,000 fathers (12% increase from 2022)
- **Uptake rate:** Approximately **56% of eligible fathers**
- Average daily payment: PLN 136.57 (€31.01) in 2018

**Parental Leave (32-week shareable):**
- **Consistently 1% of total parental leave users are fathers**
- **2016:** 4,200 fathers vs. 391,700 mothers (99% female)
- **2022:** 3,700 fathers
- **2018 data:** Women received 67% of benefit paid to men, reflecting wage disparities

### Primary Source Citations

**Legal Framework:**
Polish Labor Code (Kodeks Pracy), Articles governing parental leave. Major amendments: 2013, 2016, 2023. Available via: International Labour Organization NATLEX database.

**Uptake Statistics:**
Zakład Ubezpieczeń Społecznych [Social Insurance Institution]. (2018). Maternity, paternity and parental leave benefit statistics for 2017-2018. Warsaw: ZUS. Retrieved from lang.zus.pl

**Policy Analysis:**

## 5. JOINT PHYSICAL CUSTODY RATES IN POLAND

### STATUS: DATA NOT AVAILABLE - Critical research gap identified

### Key Finding: Official Statistics Do Not Exist

**Poland does NOT systematically collect data on joint physical custody** (opieka naprzemienna/miejsce zamieszkania). The Institute of Justice 2015 study specifically referenced could not be located.

### Available Data: Joint LEGAL Custody Only

**GUS (2019).** *Rocznik Demograficzny 2019* [Demographic Yearbook of Poland 2019]. Table 50(74): Divorces by Court Decision on Care for Minor Children in 2018, p. 246.

**Statistics (2018 divorces with minor children):**
- **Both parents retain parental authority:** 20,955 cases **(57.9%)**
- Mother only: 13,333 cases (36.8%)
- Father only: 1,276 cases (3.5%)
- Total divorces with minor children: 36,214

**Historical trend:**
- 2003: 31.4% both parents retain authority
- 2018: 57.9% both parents retain authority
- Mothers-only custody declined from 63.1% (2003) to 36.8% (2018)

**Source quality:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ Official government statistics, complete census of court records

### Critical Distinction

**What 57.9% "both parents" DOES NOT indicate:**
- Does NOT mean child lives with both parents equally
- Does NOT specify time-sharing arrangements
- Most cases likely involve: primary residence with mother, visitation for father, joint decision-making on major issues

**Legal custody (władza rodzicielska):** Decision-making authority over education, health, religion

**Physical custody (opieka naprzemienna):** Where child actually lives and sleeps, time-sharing between households

### Why Physical Custody Data Doesn't Exist

**Legal framework issues:**
1. Joint physical custody is **NOT defined** in Polish Family and Guardianship Code
2. Courts don't systematically record time-sharing schedules
3. No reporting requirement for custody arrangements
4. Legal scholars describe this as "systematic defect in the legal system"

**Academic Source:**
**Kamińska, K. (2020).** Joint Physical Custody After Parental Separation: A Polish Perspective. *Eastern European Journal of Transnational Relations*, 4(2), 87-106. DOI: 10.15290/eejtr.2020.04.02.05

- Research funded by: National Science Centre, Poland, grant no. 2019/33/N/HS5/00284
- Provides legal analysis but NO prevalence statistics
- Notes lack of empirical data

### European Comparative Context

**Hakovirta, M., Meyer, D.R., Salin, M., Lindroos, E., & Haapanen, M. (2023).** Joint physical custody of children in Europe: A growing phenomenon. *Demographic Research*, 49(18), 479-492. DOI: 10.4054/DemRes.2023.49.18

- Most comprehensive European study (EU-SILC 2021)
- Analyzed 9,102 children from 17 European countries
- **Poland explicitly NOT included** in dataset
- European average: 12.5% equal joint physical custody
- Sweden highest: 42.5% equal JPC
- Most Eastern European countries: 5% or less

### Institute of Justice Search Results

Extensive search of Instytut Wymiaru Sprawiedliwości publications found NO specific 2015 study on joint physical custody statistics. Related IWS studies exist on custody enforcement but not prevalence data.

### Source Quality Assessment

**Legal custody statistics (57.9%):** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ Official, reliable, but measures different construct

**Physical custody statistics:** ⭐ Data does not exist

**Research gap severity:** **CRITICAL** - Poland lacks fundamental data infrastructure for this measure

**Recommendation for manuscript:** State clearly that joint physical custody prevalence data is NOT collected in Poland. Can report 57.9% joint legal custody trend while noting this does not indicate actual living arrangements. Acknowledge this as significant research gap limiting international comparisons.

## 8. META-ANALYSIS: PARENTAL SENSITIVITY AND ATTACHMENT

### STATUS: COMPLETE - Full citation and statistics confirmed

### Complete Citation

**Madigan, S., Deneault, A. A., Duschinsky, R., Bakermans-Kranenburg, M. J., Schuengel, C., van IJzendoorn, M. H., Ly, A., Fearon, R. M. P., Eirich, R., & Verhage, M. L. (2024).** Maternal and paternal sensitivity: Key determinants of child attachment security examined through meta-analysis. *Psychological Bulletin*, 150(7), 839-872. https://doi.org/10.1037/bul0000433

**Publication details:**
- Journal: Psychological Bulletin (APA)
- Published: May 6, 2024 (appeared in July 2024 issue)
- Volume 150, Issue 7, Pages 839-872

### Key Effect Sizes (All from Abstract and Pages 28-30)

**Maternal Sensitivity → Attachment Security:**
- **r = 0.26**, 95% CI [0.22, 0.29]
- k = 159 studies, 202 effect sizes
- **N = 21,483 participants**
- Page location: Abstract and page 28-29

**Paternal Sensitivity → Attachment Security:**
- **r = 0.21**, 95% CI [0.14, 0.27]
- k = 22 studies, 23 effect sizes
- **N = 1,626 participants**
- Page location: Abstract and page 30

**Overall Caregivers (Combined):**
- r = 0.25, 95% CI [0.22, 0.28]
- k = 174 studies, 230 effect sizes
- **Total N = 22,914 participants**
- Page location: Abstract and page 28

### Sample Size and Scope

**Total studies included:** 181 meta-analysis studies
- 174 reported on sensitivity and security
- 159 focused on maternal sensitivity
- 22 focused on paternal sensitivity

**Comprehensive scope:** This is the most comprehensive meta-analysis on caregiver sensitivity and child attachment to date, with:
- 3x more studies than previous meta-analyses
- 6x more than original De Wolff & Van IJzendoorn (1997) meta-analysis

### Additional Effect Sizes for Insecure Attachment

**Maternal Sensitivity → Insecure Attachment Types:**
- **Avoidant attachment:** r = -0.24, 95% CI [-0.34, -0.13], k = 43 studies
- **Resistant attachment:** r = -0.12, 95% CI [-0.19, -0.06], k = 43 studies
- **Disorganized attachment:** r = -0.19, 95% CI [-0.27, -0.11], k = 24 studies
- Pages 34-36

### Key Methodological Notes

**Statistical approach:**
- Random effects meta-analyses using multilevel approach
- All effect sizes reported as Pearson correlations (r)
- Effect size interpretation (Schuengel et al., 2021): 0.10 = small, 0.20 = medium, 0.30 = large

**Heterogeneity:**
- τ² = 0.03, I² = 74.30% (all caregivers)
- Indicates substantial heterogeneity across studies

**Moderators identified:**
- Effect sizes larger with longer observation duration
- Effect sizes larger with better measurement reliability
- Association decreased as time between assessments increased
- For paternal sensitivity: larger effect sizes as children and fathers aged

**Publication bias assessment:**
- p-curve analyses suggested evidential value
- Minimal evidence of publication bias in funnel plots

**Sheri Madigan**, Department of Psychology, University of Calgary; Alberta Children's Hospital Research Institute

- **2012:** 6.4%

### Where 19% May Come From
