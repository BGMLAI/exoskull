## 3.2 Abstract - wersja robocza

```
Men die 7.3 years earlier than women in Poland, with 39,000 excess 
deaths annually. While biological factors explain approximately 
15-25% of this gap (Luy, 2003), the majority remains behaviorally 
modifiable. This paper proposes the Loop of Insufficiency Model, 
a theoretical framework explaining male excess mortality through 
gender norm transmission mechanisms.

The model advances three core hypotheses: (1) Women possess 
evolutionary mate selection power (Trivers, 1972; Buss, 1989) 
combined with structural dominance in early socialization 
institutions (94% of preschool teachers globally, ~77% of 
parental time with children); (2) Historical social structures 
functioned as male counterbalances—institutional monogamy 
limited male intrasexual competition (Henrich et al., 2012), 
while patriarchal authority compensated for fathers' reduced 
time with offspring due to high-mortality roles (Smuts, 1995); 
(3) Contemporary dissolution of these counterbalances without 
proportional reduction in female socialization dominance creates 
asymmetry that accelerates harmful norm transmission.

The framework integrates parental investment theory with gender 
socialization research to explain norm persistence despite costs. 
We specify falsification conditions and propose testable 
predictions for cross-cultural validation.

Keywords: male mortality, gender norms, parental investment, 
sexual selection, gender socialization, masculinity, evolutionary 
psychology
```

# CZĘŚĆ VI: WARUNKI FALSYFIKACJI

## 6.1 Sekcja obowiązkowa w artykule

Artykuł MUSI zawierać explicite warunki falsyfikacji. Propozycja:

> **Falsification Conditions**
> 
> The Loop of Insufficiency Model would be falsified if:
> 
> 1. **Cross-cultural test fails:** Countries with higher paternal involvement in childcare (e.g., Iceland with mandatory paternal leave) show NO reduction in male-female mortality gap compared to countries with low paternal involvement.
> 
> 2. **Historical monogamy test fails:** Societies that transitioned from polygyny to monogamy show NO reduction in male intrasexual violence and competition.
> 
> 3. **Socialization proportion is irrelevant:** The 77:23 maternal:paternal time ratio shows NO correlation with children's gender norm internalization when controlling for other variables.
> 
> 4. **Biological determinism confirmed:** Genetic and hormonal factors fully explain the mortality gap (current evidence suggests only 15-25%).
> 
> 5. **Intervention null results:** Educational interventions targeting gender norm transmission show NO effect on norm internalization or behavioral outcomes in longitudinal studies.

# CZĘŚĆ VII: ODPOWIEDZI NA KRYTYKI

## 7.1 Antycypowane zarzuty i odpowiedzi

### Zarzut 1: "To obwinia kobiety"

**Odpowiedź do włączenia:**
> "The model does not attribute blame but identifies structural patterns. Women transmit norms they themselves received through the same mechanisms. Both sexes participate in norm transmission (Endendijk et al., 2013), though institutional asymmetries create differential exposure time. The framework explicitly notes that women also suffer consequences of the system (loneliness epidemic, autoimmune conditions). Solutions require cooperation, not blame attribution."

### Zarzut 2: "To usprawiedliwia patriarchat"

**Odpowiedź:**
> "Explaining historical function differs from moral justification. Understanding why a system persisted helps design better alternatives. The model explicitly does NOT advocate returning to patriarchal structures but proposes new equilibria based on increased paternal involvement and gender-balanced education (see Implications section). This aligns with Kandiyoti's (1988) observation that women negotiated within patriarchy—recognizing bargains does not endorse them."

### Zarzut 3: "To esencjalizm biologiczny"

**Odpowiedź:**
> "The model is evolutionary but not deterministic. Following Smuts (1995), we emphasize behavioral plasticity and the potential for change. Cross-cultural variation in mortality gaps (e.g., 5 years in Sweden vs. 12 years in Russia) demonstrates that outcomes are modifiable. Biology sets parameters; culture and socialization determine where within those parameters societies fall."

### Zarzut 4: "To ideologia MRA"

**Odpowiedź:**
> "The model is grounded in mainstream evolutionary psychology (Trivers, Buss) and gender studies (Kandiyoti, Smuts), not Men's Rights activism. It does not deny historical female oppression, does not oppose feminism, and does not advocate for male dominance. The framework aims to reduce male mortality, which benefits entire families—mothers who lose sons, wives who lose husbands, children who lose fathers."

# CZĘŚĆ VIII: IMPLIKACJE PRAKTYCZNE

## 8.1 Sekcja "Implications for Intervention"

Artykuł powinien kończyć się praktycznymi implikacjami:

> **Implications for Intervention**
> 
> If the Loop of Insufficiency Model is valid, effective interventions must target norm transmission mechanisms rather than individual behavior change alone. We propose:
> 
> 1. **Increased paternal involvement policies:** Mandatory paternal leave (Iceland model: 6 months reserved for fathers) increases father-child contact time, potentially rebalancing transmission asymmetry.
> 
> 2. **Gender diversification in early education:** Targeted recruitment of male preschool and primary teachers (currently 3-6% globally) could provide alternative models of masculinity.
> 
> 3. **Conscious transmission education:** Training programs for parents and educators about implicit gender bias in socialization (following Thomassin et al., 2019 findings).
> 
> 4. **Updated "behavioral algorithm":** Public health messaging that reframes masculinity norms as culturally constructed and modifiable, rather than biologically fixed.
> 
> These interventions require empirical testing through randomized controlled trials measuring norm internalization, behavioral outcomes, and ultimately mortality indicators.
